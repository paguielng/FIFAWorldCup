1. FIFA Odyssey

  2. 1930 - Uruguay

    The inaugural World Cup, hosted and won by Uruguay, where a new era of international football began.

  3. 1934 - Italy

    A politically charged tournament where Italy's dominance set the tone for early World Cups.

  4. 1938 - France

    A pre-war showcase of European football with Italy defending their title in grand style.

  2. 1950 - Brazil

    The return of the World Cup after WWII, remembered for Uruguay’s shocking triumph over Brazil in the Maracanazo.

  3. 1954 - Switzerland

    The "Miracle of Bern," where West Germany shocked Hungary’s "Golden Team" in a thrilling 3-2 final, with Switzerland hosting the highest-scoring World Cup match ever (Austria 7-5 Switzerland).

  4. 1958 - Sweden

    A star was born as 17-year-old Pelé dazzled the world and Brazil claimed their first title.

  2. 1962 - Chile

    Brazil’s resilience shone again in a tournament marked by gritty matches and individual brilliance.

  3. 1966 - England

    The host nation triumphed with Geoff Hurst's historic hat-trick in the final, forever debated for its controversial goals.

  4. 1970 - Mexico

    Brazil’s golden generation, led by Pelé, delivered perhaps the greatest team performance in football history.

  2. 1974 - West Germany

    The rise of total football and the crowning of West Germany after an epic clash with the Netherlands.

  3. 1978 - Argentina

    The hosts capitalized on home advantage to secure a controversial but historic victory.

  4. 1982 - Spain

    Italy’s Paolo Rossi stole the show in a World Cup remembered for thrilling drama and high-scoring matches.

  2. 1986 - Mexico

    Diego Maradona’s genius, the "Hand of God," and the "Goal of the Century" defined this unforgettable tournament.

  3. 1990 - Italy

    A defensive World Cup, where West Germany edged Argentina in a tense, tactical final.

  4. 1994 - USA

    The World Cup goes global with Brazil’s penalty shootout victory over Italy in a sun-drenched spectacle.

  2. 1998 - France

    Zinedine Zidane’s brilliance and the host nation’s triumph against Brazil marked a golden moment for Les Bleus.

  3. 2002 - South Korea/Japan

    The first Asian World Cup, with Brazil's Ronaldo redemption and surprise runs by South Korea and Turkey.

  4. 2006 - Germany

    Italy's defensive mastery and Zidane’s infamous headbutt created an unforgettable finale.

  2. 2010 - South Africa

    The first African World Cup, celebrated for its vibrant culture, and Spain’s tiki-taka brilliance.

  3. 2014 - Brazil

    Germany’s historic 7-1 demolition of Brazil and Mario Götze’s extra-time winner in the final.

  4. 2018 - Russia

    France’s dynamic squad shone, winning a thrilling final and signaling the rise of young stars like Mbappé.
